What to Expect

Here's how this works, step by step:

1. You Share (When Ready)

Write or call whenever you feel ready. There's no pressure, no deadline. Share as much or as little as you need.

2. I Listen (Really Listen)

I read every word carefully. I don't judge, analyze, or try to fix. I just listen with my whole attention.

3. You Feel Heard

Sometimes being truly heard is enough. If you want a gentle response, I'll offer one. If you just need to be heard, that's perfect too.

Response Time

I typically respond within 24-48 hours. For calls, I'll return them when I can give you my full, undivided attention.

Who Am I?

I'm Pragadeesh, and I've always been the quiet one. The one who felt too much, but never spoke up. I understand what it's like to carry emotions that feel too heavy to share.

I created this space because I believe everyone deserves to be heard — really heard — without judgment or the pressure to be "fixed." Sometimes we just need someone to witness our truth.

I'm not a therapist, but I am someone who listens with my whole heart. I'm here for you.

Gentle Reminders

If you're in crisis

Please reach out to crisis support immediately. You deserve immediate help and support.

Your feelings are valid

Whatever you're experiencing right now is real and important. You don't need to minimize or justify how you feel.

Take your time

There's no rush. Share when you're ready, in your own words, at your own pace.

Questions You Might Have

What if I don't know what to say?

That's completely okay. You can start with "I don't know what to say" or just share whatever is on your mind. There's no right or wrong way to reach out.

Do I have to share personal details?

Not at all. Share only what feels comfortable. You can be completely anonymous if you prefer.

What if I change my mind after reaching out?

You can always let me know if you'd prefer I don't respond, or if you need space. Your comfort and boundaries always come first.

Is this professional therapy?

No, this isn't therapy. I'm just someone who listens. If you need professional help, I encourage you to reach out to a mental health professional.
